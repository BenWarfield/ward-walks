We enjoyed our farmhouse breakfast at the Dairy House Farm

and then set off. A couple of hours later we were crossing the Wye again, this time in Bredwardine. We were still in England, by the way. The bridge, built in 1769, has those typical cutwaters, part of the bridge construction but handy for pedestrians who need to duck out of the way of cars.

For the next several hours we were climbing a hill, Merbach Hill. There were many fields, and many good views.

Thann must have been reading his map, trying to figure which direction to take to get out of the field:

Soon after noon we were at the trig point on top of Merbach Hill:

 

The afternoon was pleasant, and by 5:00 we were in Hay-on-Wye. We were comforted to see our first sign in Welsh - though we didn't have a dog to worry about:  

We happily and excitedly walked through the familiar streets to the B&B where we've stayed before, "Rest for the Tired".

We had booked two nights, to have a rest day there. We walked up to the Castle Bookshop, with its outside bookshelves:

We just relaxed the next day, doing not much except for visiting six or eight (or ten?) bookshops and the outdoor shop. We managed to buy only five or six small books. And we did a load of clothes at the laundromat. And we had warm-from-the-oven scones at an excellent tea shop. Here's Thann getting cash from Barclay's, and the sign for the Granary Cafe where we had dinner, with the town clock beyond:

We admired the restoration work that our host, John Thomas, is doing on the building behind their house. They hope to retire from the B&B business in a few years and live in the house John is restoring.

 

It was a perfect rest day!
