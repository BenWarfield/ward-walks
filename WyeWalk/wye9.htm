Wye Valley Walk - Newbridge to Rhyd-y-Benwch

This was our final day of the Wye Valley Walk. The view back to Llangurig when we crossed over the Wye the following morning was most appealing.

After our almost standard hill-climbing beginning to the day, in an hour or so we descended again to the Wye. It was dwindling, but still a river.

There were a few last stiles to cross:

 

and then we entered a large farmyard. We were decidedly put off by the sign beside the farm track as we entered:

After reading this, we had second thoughts about our plans to sit and eat lunch there. We walked for a couple of hours on their farm tracks. The farm, as well as being a huge sheep farm, is the "Sweet Lamb Motorsport Complex". There are frequent car and motorcycle rallies held here. For the first few miles we were beside the Wye for the final time. It was now definitely a stream rather than a river. Here are the last pictures of our friendly Wye River:

The farm covers miles and miles of the lower slopes of Plynlimon. Plynlimon is a mountain covering a large area, though it has no spectacular peaks. In Welsh, it's Pumlumon (the Welsh "u" sounds like the English short i), and pum means five. There are five peaks, though they look more like rounded hilltops. Five rivers have their source on Plynlimon, including the Severn and the Rheidol, as well as the Wye. I had expected an afternoon of mountain-climbing, but the whole day was just gentle walking. We went through sheep pens:

and passed the ruins of a silver mine:

before finally looking up into the hills toward the source of the Wye:

Our path now left the Wye and headed off through a forest, the Hafren Forest. Then in an odd twist, we reached the infant Severn River. When you read about the Severn Bridges, usually that they're closed because of high winds, the newspapers are referring to the mile-long bridges down south for the M4 and M48 motorways. Our "Severn Bridge" was considerably more peaceful!

 

We were thus walking for a half-mile on the Severn Way, which we could have followed another 210 miles. But we didn't. So there you have it - the final bit of our Wye Valley Walk was beside the Severn!
